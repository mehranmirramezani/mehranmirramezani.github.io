Research Overview

AI-Empowered Engineering Problem-Solving: The overarching vision of my research centers on translation of computational mechanics and machine learning into modern engineering problem-solving and effective decision-making through artificial intelligence across a wide range of applications, including: health and bioscience, engineering design, material sciences, digital twins, etc.

Learning-Based Modeling and Design

This research explores novel learning-based modeling paradigms integrating computational mechanics, machine learning architectures, and optimization techniques to facilitate and automate the design of subject-specific mechanical systems with complex functionalities.

Multifunctional Mechanical Systems Design

The central question we try to answer is can we design an intelligent system combining a control system and a mechanical body (structure) such that the intelligent mechanical system performs a set of tasks efficiently? To address this question, we developed a framework inspired by biological systems known as mechanical intelligence whereby biological systems co-evolve their body and cognitive control system to efficiently perform their daily tasks. We developed machine learning analogs of this process in which neural network (control system) parametrs and the pores of a meta-material (body) are jointly optimized to reduce a loss function defining a set of tasks.

Learning Symmetric Meta-Materials

The periodic pore structures of meta-materials can be manipulated to achieve desired functionalities. Existing approaches are limited to translational symmetry of a pore unit cell. However, exploiting other spatial patterns can potentially unlock meta-materials with unexpected responses. We developed an ML framework for learning meta-materials that exhibit any 17 2D crystallographic symmetry. This is achieved by transforming a reference geometry according to a neural network flow that is volume/symmetry-preserving. Coupling the NN architecture with a differentiable mechanics simulator enabled the design of meta-materials with exotic behaviors (e.g., negative Poisson's ratio), also verified via fabrication.

Cardiovascular Mechanics Modeling

Cardiovascular mechanics simulations can uncover functional indicators of cardiovascular diseases (CVD)—the leading cause of death worldwide—not revealed through imaging, the primary technology for CVD diagnosis. This research enables development of cardiovascular digital twins model for noninvasive and ex situ personalized healthcare decision-making and treatment planning.

Reduced Order Modeling of Blood Flow

Image-based CFD simulations are the modeling standard to study patient-specific blood flow features in the cardiovascular system. However, these simulations are time-consuming and challenging, which prohibit their applications for timely clinical decision-making and perform parametric analyses. Therefore, we developed a phyiscs-based fluid dynamics reduced order model (ROM)—arguably one of the most effective ROM strategies developed to-date. We leveraged fundamental fluid mechanics principles to propose an ODE system as a lumped parameter (LP) governing equation by considering various sources of energy dissipation of blood flow. We also project the results of our ROM approach back to the 3D model.

Virtual Treatment Planning via Digital Twins

One of the common procedures to improve stenotic arteries is stenting. In patients with multiple stenotic lesions it can be difficult to predict where to treat in order to maximize benefit and reduce risk. We developed an interactive digital twins framework to perform various virtual stenting scenarios, which can help clinicians to choose best treatment strategies. An application example to improve coronary artery disease treatment planning is presented in a software demo. Note: FFR, coronary pressure normalized with aortic pressure, is the disease indicator where lesions with FFR less than 0.8 are flow limiting.

Computational Modeling of Thrombosis

This research focuses on understanding blood clot formation and growth under flow conditions by developing computational tools combining physics-based approach, data-driven techniques, and in-vivo and ex-vivo experimental data.
